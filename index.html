About Me


My name is Jacquelyne Mido Glover. I go by Jack for short. I was born in Frankfurt, Germany to a military family with strong values of open mindedness, hard work, loyalty, patriotism and adaptation.

I want to get my foot in the door in the growing tech industry as a JavaScript developer, creating functional code for web apps.

I am an artist and a writer. I live for the thrill of producing something from nothing. There is no more magical feeling in the world than to create.

The University of Colorado at Boulder is my Alma mater. There I graduated as a summa cum laude with a Bachelorâ€™s of Arts in English.

I've worked as a staff writer at Metrosphere Magazine where my focus was on art and art events in the Denver Metro area. I researched different artists and movements, wrote articles, and worked with a team to edit and revise articles into a final draft by the publication deadline. I also worked as the program coordinator for a non-profit teen mentorship program in Idaho Springs called the Clear Creek Rock House. My work at the Rock House was almost entirely self-directed: I had the goal of producing a mentorship program that utilized best practices to enrich the lives of underprivileged teens.

Currently I am a student at the University of Denver Coding Boot Camp. I have learned HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and jQuery. I have also learned how to write ajax requests, use gitHub, integrate APIs, utilize frameworks like Bootstrap and Materialize, and use node.js. In the coming weeks I will learn ReactJS, Angular, MySQL, and MongoDB.
